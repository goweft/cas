## Notes

- The memory drift is the most important learning of this session.
  The starting context confidently described work that hadn't
  happened. Catching it required actually running git log and grep,
  not asking. Future sessions should audit before relying on any
  "Phase N" or "X is committed" claim from memory.
- The concept doc's value didn't depend on the phantom Phase 1 being
  real — the idea stands on its own. But the dependency chain section
  was actively misleading until it got rewritten. Worth remembering
  that the parts of a doc most likely to be wrong are the parts that
  reach for narrative continuity with surrounding context.

  `roadmap.md` (what to do next), `concepts/app-ingestion.md` (major
  future idea), session logs (`session-2026-04-05.md`,
  `session-2026-04-26.md`). The pattern is working.

  protecting in future revisions. Removing it would re-enable the
  same pattern that produced the phantom — planning ahead of reality.
